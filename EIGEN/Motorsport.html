Formula 1

Red Bull Racing is one of the most dominant and innovative teams in Formula 1, known for its speed, bold strategy, and cutting-edge engineering. Backed by the world-famous energy drink brand, the team brings a unique mix of flair, precision, and relentless competitiveness to the sport. Since entering the grid in 2005, Red Bull has built a reputation for challenging the traditional giants of F1—and winning.

In Formula 1, Red Bull operates its own racing team, based in Milton Keynes, UK, and competes at the highest level of motorsport. The team designs and builds its cars, collaborates closely with engine suppliers (currently Honda via Red Bull Powertrains), and manages a world-class lineup of drivers. Red Bull's aggressive development strategy and data-driven decision making have led to multiple world championships, most recently with star driver Max Verstappen. World Rally championship

Red Bull is a powerful force in the world of rally racing, bringing adrenaline, extreme performance, and fearless energy to the dirt, gravel, snow, and tarmac stages across the globe. As a major supporter and promoter of rally events and teams, Red Bull has become deeply intertwined with the sport’s culture of daring speed and off-road mastery.

In rally racing, Red Bull partners with top teams and elite drivers competing in events like the FIA World Rally Championship (WRC) and Dakar Rally. Red Bull also runs its own rally programs and junior development initiatives, investing in the future of the sport. Whether it's tight forest stages or brutal desert runs, Red Bull’s presence adds intensity, style, and a commitment to pushing the limits of what's possible behind the wheel. MotoGP

Red Bull powers through the world of MotoGP with speed, precision, and high-octane energy, backing some of the sport’s most competitive teams and riders. In the fastest motorcycle racing championship on Earth, Red Bull’s presence is felt in the paddock, on the track, and in the development of the sport’s rising stars.

Through its partnership with KTM Factory Racing and sponsorship of standout riders like Brad Binder and Pedro Acosta, Red Bull is deeply involved in MotoGP and its support series, including Moto2 and Moto3. The brand also operates the Red Bull Rookies Cup, a critical stepping stone for young talent aiming to reach the world stage. By fueling both champions and future legends, Red Bull continues to leave a bold mark on two-wheel racing at the highest level.

Motocross

Red Bull goes full throttle in motocross, a sport where grit, speed, and gravity-defying jumps define every race. As one of the most physically demanding motorsports, motocross is a natural fit for Red Bull’s commitment to extreme athleticism and bold performance.

Red Bull sponsors top-tier teams like Red Bull KTM Factory Racing and legendary riders such as Jeffrey Herlings and Cooper Webb. With deep roots in both AMA and MXGP championships, the brand pushes the sport forward through innovation, media, and spectacular events. Air racing

Red Bull took motorsports to the skies with the Red Bull Air Race, combining aviation skill, insane speeds, and extreme precision in a one-of-a-kind aerial competition. Pilots fly lightweight, agile planes through low-altitude courses marked by inflatable pylons — just meters above the ground and often inches from disaster.

Though the official championship ended in 2019, Red Bull’s impact on the world of air racing is lasting. It helped shape a new kind of motorsport that blended athleticism, engineering, and showmanship. Today, Red Bull continues to support aerial events and daredevil aviation through stunt flying and aerobatic exhibitions around the globe.
